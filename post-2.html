Biafo Glacier

The Biafo Glacier is a 67 km (42 mi) long glacier in the Karakoram Mountains of Gilgit Baltistan, Pakistan which meets the 49 km (30 mi) long Hispar Glacier at an altitude of 5,128 m (16,824 ft) at Hispar La (Pass) to create the world's longest glacial system outside the polar regions.


This parkway of ice interfaces two antiquated mountain kingdoms, Nagar (quickly south of Hunza) in the west with Baltistan in the east. The navigate utilizes 51 of the Biafo Glacier's 67 km and the greater part of the Hispar Glacier to shape a 100 km (62 mi) icy course.


The Biafo Glacier gives a trekker a few days of exceptionally strenuous, frequently wild stone jumping, with fantastic perspectives all through and Snow Lake close to the high point. Snow Lake, comprising of parts of the upper Biafo Glacier and its tributary icy mass Sim Gang, is one of the world's biggest bowls of snow or ice on the planet outside the polar areas, up to 1,600 m (0.99 mi) inside and out.

The Biafo Glacier is the world's third longest icy mass outside the polar districts, second just to the 70 km (43 mi) Siachen Glacier controlled by India and Tajikistan's 77 km (48 mi) long Fedchenko Glacier.


Campgrounds along the Biafo are situated off of the ice sheet, adjoining the sidelong moraines and steep mountainsides. The initial three (heading up from the last town before the ice sheet, the thousand-year-old Askole town) are wonderful locales with streaming water adjacent. Mango and Namla, the initial two campgrounds, are frequently secured in blooms and Namla has a stunning waterfall exceptionally close to the outdoors zone. Baintha, the third campground, is regularly utilized as a rest day. A vast green knoll, it has a couple running streams close to the camp and numerous spots to spend the day rock climbing or rappelling.


Proof of untamed life can be seen all through the trek. The Ibex and the Markhor Mountain Goat can be found and the range is celebrated for chestnut bears and snow panthers, despite the fact that sightings are uncommon.
